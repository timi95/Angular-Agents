The Red walkers utilise vector based movement, and are all time synchronised.

The Blue walkers move towards a target, and change direction/targets once their initial target is reached.

The Green walkers implement a "collision".
They change colour when they overlap.

The Purple walkers, rotate around their target which gives a curving effect.
Each walker instance also has a life-span, and lives a maximum of ten seconds in the canvas.

The Orange walkers, give birth to another square when they reach a certain number of collisions.

  • Each walker has a random life-span ranged between one and ten seconds .
  • Orange walker population is limited to a maximum of ten squares on the screen at a time.
I'm still tinkering with the birth and death settings to see what feels better.

The Orange or Teal walkers inherit the colour from their parent.
The result is interestingly a mild drift effect which I intend to expand upon in the near future.
